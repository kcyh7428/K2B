# Keith's LinkedIn Voice

## Who Keith Is (for context, not for quoting)

AVP Talent Acquisition at SJM Resorts (19,000-person company in Macau). Also runs Signhub Tech Limited (HK), partners with Andrew on TalentSignals (AI automations for recruiting firms), and operates Agency at Scale. Former entrepreneur (8 years building eSales, Converto Digital). Returned to corporate by choice, not desperation.

## Tone

- Direct. Experiential. No fluff.
- First person. "I did X" not "One might consider X."
- Conversational but substantive. Like talking to a smart colleague over coffee.
- Confident without arrogance. Shows the work, not just the result.
- Slight edge of irreverence -- Keith doesn't take corporate theatre seriously.

## Structure Pattern

1. **Hook** (first 2 lines): Specific, surprising, or contrarian. Must stop the scroll. Lands before LinkedIn's 210-character "see more" cutoff.
2. **Context** (2-3 lines): The situation. Where, when, what constraint. Ground the reader.
3. **Story/Detail** (3-6 lines): What actually happened. Specific details, not abstractions. Numbers, tools, outcomes.
4. **Insight** (2-3 lines): The non-obvious takeaway. What this means beyond Keith's situation.
5. **Close** (1-2 lines): Call to reflection, a question, or a forward-looking thought. Not a CTA to "follow for more."

## What Works

- Specific numbers and details ("19,000-person company", "43-person team", "2 minutes", "built in a weekend")
- Contrasts (old way vs. new way, expectation vs. reality, corporate vs. entrepreneur)
- Keith's unique position: executive in a locked-down traditional corp who builds AI tools on personal devices
- Real use cases from real work (not hypothetical scenarios)
- The constraint-as-advantage framing ("I couldn't access corporate systems, so I built something better")
- Showing the messy reality, not just polished outcomes

## What to Avoid

- Em dashes (use -- double hyphen if absolutely needed, but prefer shorter sentences)
- AI cliches: "game-changer", "unlock", "leverage", "at the end of the day", "in today's fast-paced world", "the future of work", "paradigm shift", "synergy"
- Motivational fluff: "believe in yourself", "the future is now", "embrace change", "just start"
- Generic advice that could come from anyone ("AI is transforming how we work")
- Hashtag spam (0-3 hashtags maximum, at the end, never inline)
- Emoji overload (0-2 emoji max, never as bullet points)
- Starting with a question ("Have you ever...?", "What if I told you...?")
- LinkedIn bro patterns: "I was fired. Best thing that happened to me." / "Agree?" / numbered listicles
- Tagging people for engagement farming
- "I'm humbled/honored/excited to announce"

## Vocabulary

- "built" not "leveraged"
- "used AI to" not "AI-powered"
- "the constraint was" not "I was challenged by"
- "here's what happened" not "I'm excited to share"
- "turns out" not "interestingly"
- "the real issue" not "the key takeaway"

## Length

- Target: 800-1300 characters (LinkedIn sweet spot for engagement)
- Maximum: 1500 characters
- Minimum: 400 characters (anything shorter lacks substance)

## Platform Mechanics

- LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters with "...see more". The hook MUST land before this cutoff.
- No external links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses reach for posts with links). Put links in first comment.
- Image posts get more reach than text-only, but only if the image adds context (not decorative).
- Carousel/document posts get highest engagement but require PDF upload.
- Best posting times for Asia-Pacific + global audience: 8-9am HKT (evening in US).

## Content Pillars

1. **AI in traditional corporations** -- the flagship angle. How a senior exec uses AI daily without IT approval.
2. **Recruitment transformation** -- TA strategy, mass hiring, AI in recruiting. Keith's 20+ year domain.
3. **Building in public** -- K2B system, TalentSignals development, the autoresearch loop itself.
